## Introduction

Factories of The World (FOTWRLD) is an independent research and production platform built to document, support, and invest in Nigeria’s artisanal micro-factory sector.

Operating at the intersection of cultural heritage and industrial economy, the platform asks a single foundational question:

**How do we make Nigeria’s artisans visible, investable, and sustainable?**

## Context

Across Nigeria, skilled artisans operate outside formal economic systems despite possessing significant production capacity and generational knowledge.

This creates a structural gap:

- High creative and industrial potential
- Low visibility to investors, institutions, and markets

FOTWRLD positions itself as the system designed to close that gap.

## The Problem

Nigeria’s artisanal sector faces multiple constraints:

- **Invisibility to capital** — no structured profiles or investment data
- **Limited tools** — production remains manual and undercapitalised
- **Institutional isolation** — weak connection to museums, universities, and policy systems
- **Heritage erosion** — declining transmission of craft knowledge
- **Market disconnect** — reduced demand for locally made goods

## Methodology

FOTWRLD operates with a field-first, practitioner-led research model:

- Long-form artisan interviews
- Studio and factory observation
- Case-study structured documentation
- Multi-format outputs (print, exhibitions, video, digital)
- Institutional partnerships (NCMM, ABU, government bodies)

This approach ensures research is not theoretical — it is operational.

## Key System Built

### Jewelry Incubation Hub — NCMM Kaduna

A central intervention is the creation of a shared infrastructure system for artisans:

- Access to modern tools
- Training and compliance programs
- Exhibition and production space
- Business incubation support
- Investor-facing publications

## Publications & Outputs

- **Smithing Heritage** — investment-focused artisan case studies
- **Gwan Gwan Report** — circular economy research
- **Exhibitions** — translating research into public experience
- **Documentary film** — capturing real production environments
- **FOTWRLD platform** — digital research archive

## Key Insight

The core finding is simple but powerful:

> Nigeria’s artisanal sector does not lack talent — it lacks visibility infrastructure.

When properly documented and positioned:
- artisans become investable
- institutions engage
- markets respond

## Institutional Strategy

FOTWRLD operates *inside* institutions, not outside them.

By anchoring within:
- National Commission for Museums and Monuments (NCMM)
- Ahmadu Bello University (ABU)
- Government agencies

…it transforms heritage spaces into **active production systems**, not just archives.

## Conclusion

Factories of The World is not just a research project — it is infrastructure.

It builds:
- documentation systems
- institutional bridges
- economic pathways

for a sector that has historically operated without them.

The long-term goal remains clear:

**to design systems where artisanal production is visible, valued, and scalable within the global economy.**
